Mobile App Design

Description

In this course, students will learn to create web-based interactive applications for mobile devices. They will apply advanced Web design and interactive programming techniques to produce applications that are compatible with a variety of mobile platforms. They will learn writing and design strategies to produce content optimized for mobile devices. They will be introduced to software and protocols for converting their Web-based applications to "native" applications designed to run on specific platforms and devices.

Objectives

  1. to demonstrate competency in advanced HTML, CSS, and interactive programming languages relevant to Web-based mobile application development;
  2. to apply effective design and writing strategies in creating content optimized for delivery on mobile devices;
  3. to use programming languages such as JavaScript to produce effective user interfaces;
  4. to demonstrate basic knowledge of the field of mobile application development, including leading device manufacturers and operating systems, common development protocols, and career paths.

What's New

PhoneGap

Nov. 26, 2012

Check out chapter 11 in the Firtman book for information on downloading PhoneGap and packaging your app for distribution for various platforms. Refer to chapter 8, if necessary, for helping in creating a Cache Manifest file.

Create folders for each "native" app you wish to create. Copy all important files for your apps into each folder, and remember to cull files and sub-folders you don't need to include.

You can download PhoneGap at phonegap.com

Go full-screen

Oct. 29, 2012

Check out chapter 8 in the Firtman book for information on making your app full-screen. There are some important <meta> tags that must be included in the head. Also, you need to enable the fullscreen attribute in each page area and set each header and footer to "fixed" position.

Note that going full-screen will change the behavior of your navigation. It can disable your back buttons and the header will cover the top portion of your content until the user taps the screen.

Policies

Academic integrity

Cheating in any form will not be tolerated. Students caught violating any aspect of the University of Tampa's Academic Integrity Policy will be penalized in all cases. Penalty ranges from "0" on an assignment to "F" for the course without regard to a student's accumulated points. Students may also face expulsion. It is the student's responsibility to become familiar with the policies of the university regarding academic integrity and to avoid violating such policies.

Interaction with others

The college classroom is one of the most important venues our society provides for the free and critical exchange of ideas. I encourage you to take advantage of this space as an opportunity to explore and to challenge your own ideas and those of others, but I also expect you to do so in a manner that demonstrates respect for your classmates, for me, and for this university. You must participate in class discussion, e-mail, and other exchanges in a responsible manner, and you should grant others the same courtesy when they express their opinions that you would expect from them when you express your own.

Cell phones, notebook computers, and other electronic devices

Modern communication devices are ubiquitous, handy, and—sometimes—even valuable learning tools in college classrooms. Often, however, they distract us from the task at hand and annoy those around us. As a rule, please turn off your cell phones, portable music players, and other devices, and leave them off for the duration of the class period unless I specifically say it's OK to use them. Setting your phone to vibrate or using it only for texting are not the same as turning it off. You are welcome to use your laptop during class, but please don't use it for anything not related to class.

UT's Class Disruption Policy

Every student has the right to a comfortable learning environment where the open and honest exchange of ideas may freely occur. Each student is expected to do his or her part to ensure that the classroom (and anywhere else the class may meet) remains conducive to learning. According to the terms of the University of Tampa Disruption Policy, the professor will take immediate action when inappropriate behavior occurs.

ADA compliance

Any student who has special needs because of a disability should go directly to the Academic Center for Excellence in North Walker Hall. Students may phone 813-258-7251, or e-mail jdelvalle@ut.edu to report their needs and provide documentation of their disabilities for certification. Jennifer Del Valle is the associate director of the Academic Center for Excellence, Student Disability Services. Please feel free to discuss this issue in private if you need more information.

Class interruption due to adverse conditions

In case of any adverse weather conditions or other situations which could interrupt the schedule of our classes, each student is asked to access www.ut.edu for information about the status of the campus and class meetings. In addition, please refer to ut.blackboard.com for announcements and other important information. You are responsible for accessing this information.
